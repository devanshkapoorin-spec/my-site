# Meera Nambiar — Target Customer Review

*I'm a hiring manager at a mid-sized consulting firm in Singapore. I look at maybe 40 candidate profiles a week. I opened this site from a LinkedIn message. I have 90 seconds.*

---

## My honest walkthrough

**Second 0–5:** Nice site. Clean. Not the usual Notion export or Wix template. I'm staying.

**Second 5–15:** "I've never been satisfied just describing a market." Okay, I like this person already. "Three countries. Four sectors." Good. I'm reading.

**Second 15–25:** "Let's work together." I hover over it. It goes to a contact form. I don't fill in contact forms for candidates I haven't screened yet. I want to see the CV first. There's no CV link. I look for one. It's not here.

**Second 25–40:** I scroll past a moving ticker of skills. I've already seen those skills mentioned in the paragraph above. This adds nothing. I keep scrolling.

**Second 40–55:** About section. Two long paragraphs. I'm skimming. I catch "Jakarta," "Andhra Pradesh and Telangana," "KPMG." Good signals. But I still don't know: what's his degree classification? What tools does he know specifically? The sidebar says "Finance · Consulting · Tech · Policy" — that's too broad. I need him to tell me what he's best at.

**Second 55–75:** Services section. "Research & Policy Analysis, Data & Economic Analysis, Financial Modelling & Strategy." Okay. But these are things every economics graduate claims. What I want to see: what does the actual output look like? Show me a brief. Show me a model. Show me anything.

**Second 75–90:** Projects. Four projects. No links. No PDFs. I read the Carbon Credit one because it sounds relevant to a client we're pitching. "Built not to describe the market, but to help navigate it." Okay, but I can't see the model. I can't share it with my team. This is a dead end.

---

## Questions I still have after 90 seconds

1. Where is the resume? Why isn't there a download link?
2. "Affects 5M+ users" — what does this mean exactly? He advocated for a gaming company. Is that relevant to consulting?
3. Which of these three capability areas is he actually strongest at? They're all presented with equal weight. I need to know where to put him on a team.
4. "Available May 2026" — can we talk to him now? What about a summer position before graduation?
5. The projects are interesting but I can't share them or look at the underlying work. Are these published anywhere?
6. I notice there are no references, no testimonials, nothing from anyone who has worked with him. Why should I trust these claims?

---

## What would make me convert

One thing: a resume download button. I would download it, forward it to my team, and send a reply LinkedIn message — all in under 2 minutes. Without it, I bookmark the site (maybe) and move on.

Second thing: a visible, downloadable work sample. Even one. A 2-page brief, a one-pager on the pricing index methodology, a model schematic. Anything I can hold.

**Likelihood to contact: 30%.** I'm intrigued but I can't move forward without the CV. Most people in my position won't wait.
