# Rahul Khanna — Angel Investor / Credibility Review

*I'm not literally investing in Devansh. But I'm evaluating him the same way I evaluate a founder deck: does this person have a distinctive angle, and do I believe the claims?*

---

## The credibility audit

Let me go through the claims and stress-test them.

**"Policy briefs that reached state governments affecting 5M+ users"**
This is the strongest claim on the site. Games 24x7 is a real company. Andhra Pradesh and Telangana gaming regulation is a real issue. I believe this. But the phrasing "supporting advocacy" is soft — did the briefs change anything? If yes, say so. If you can't say so, "informing strategy at a company with 5M+ users" is still credible.

**"Financial models that survived KPMG audits"**
This is well-worded. KPMG is a trust signal. I believe it. Good.

**"$500M World Bank initiative independently evaluated"**
This is a student research project. It is NOT the same as being hired by the World Bank to evaluate a programme. The framing implies more than is accurate. A sophisticated reader — the kind Devansh is targeting — will notice this. Call it what it is: "independent academic evaluation." Overselling kills credibility faster than underselling.

**"Pricing index covering ~80% of Jakarta's used-car market"**
Specific, concrete, verifiable in principle. Good.

**"Merged 300,000+ EU ETS permit records"**
This is a project claim. It's plausible for a student working in Stata or Python. I believe it. The "built to inform active trading strategy" framing is doing some work — whose trading strategy? Was it actually used? This is borderline.

## The positioning problem

This site presents Devansh as a generalist who has done everything. That's fine for a job application. It's not fine for being memorable.

The three-panel capabilities structure — Policy, Data, Finance — signals: I can do a bit of everything. That's table stakes for an economics graduate. The recruiter reading this thinks: "Smart kid, varied experience, probably put in a team doing research support."

What's missing: a clear answer to "what's the angle?" The AI interests section gets closest: "what does this actually change about how economists work?" — that question, expanded, could be a genuine differentiator. An economics student who is seriously thinking about AI-augmented analysis is rare. But it's buried at the bottom of the hobbies section.

**The single biggest credibility gap:** there are four projects with specific, impressive methodology descriptions and exactly zero links, downloads, or verifiable outputs. To a skeptical reader, this is a yellow flag. A researcher who is proud of their work and confident in its quality would share it. The absence suggests either the work isn't sharable (fine, say that) or it doesn't exist as a standalone document (less fine). Either way, one downloadable work sample would immediately resolve this.

## What I'd bet on

The hero copy signals someone with genuine intellectual character. The interests section shows range. The cricket VP story is my favourite thing here — it's an example of the same build-from-nothing quality he claims in his work, proved through a non-work context. That's exactly the kind of cross-domain signal I look for.

The site is promising. It's not yet credible enough for the confidence it projects. Fix the verification problem — link the work, link the CV, qualify the $500M claim — and the confidence becomes earned rather than asserted.

**Credibility score: 7/10.** Would hold, wouldn't commit yet.
