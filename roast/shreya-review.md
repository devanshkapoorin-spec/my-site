# Shreya Iyer — Brand Strategist Review

**First impression:** Aesthetically, I'm into it. The editorial light scheme, the Playfair Display, the gold. It looks like something. But looking memorable and *being* memorable are different problems, and this site solves the first while largely ignoring the second.

---

## What works

The headline — "I've never been satisfied just describing a market" — is the best line on the page. It has a point of view. It implies a frustration, a standard, a character. I remember it.

The stats row is smart. 5M+ users, $500M evaluated — these are numbers that stop a scroll. The international thread (India, Indonesia, US) is genuinely differentiating and the site surfaces it well in the hero subtext.

The interests section is the most human thing here. The football research line — "the most fun I've had with a dataset" — is the second-best line on the page. More of that, everywhere.

---

## What doesn't

**The copy goes cold after the hero.** The headline promises a person with an edge. The About section delivers... a competent LinkedIn bio. "Closing that gap requires rigour, range, and the willingness to build the framework" — that's three abstract nouns in a row. That's not how a person with an edge talks. That's how someone summarising their edge talks.

**The pull quote is generic.** "The gap I've always been drawn to: between what economic theory says should happen and what the data shows is actually happening." Every economics graduate is drawn to this gap. It's the definition of applied economics. This quote should be something only Devansh could say — a specific take, a specific moment, a specific market. Instead it's a mission statement.

**He has no opinions.** I read every word of this site and I still don't know what Devansh *thinks* about anything. Does he think the carbon credit market is broken? Does he think AI regulation in Europe is overcorrecting? Does he have a take on how the Indian used-car market will evolve? He has studied all of these things. There is nothing here. A brand without a POV is just a resume with better fonts.

**Services section is misframed.** He's not a consultant. Calling these "capabilities" or "what I bring" is fine — but the headers (Research & Policy Analysis, Data & Economic Analysis, Financial Modelling & Strategy) sound exactly like the categories on every generic analyst resume. The writing inside the cards is stronger but the framing undercuts it.

**The interests section is buried.** The most human part of this site is section 5 of 6. By the time a busy recruiter gets there, they've already made a decision. The personality needs to be in the first scroll, not the fifth.

---

## The one thing I'd fix first

Inject a specific opinion somewhere in the hero or about section. Not "I think markets are complex." Something defensible. Something that makes someone disagree or nod hard. Right now, the site is credible but forgettable. The headline promises a person. The rest of the site delivers a profile.

**Memorability score: 6/10.** The design sticks. The person doesn't — yet.
