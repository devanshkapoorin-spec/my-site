# Ankit Verma — Growth Marketer Review

**5-second test result:** "Economics student, available next year, wants an analyst job." Passable. Not sharp. Not converted.

---

## The funnel as I see it

```
Landing → Hero → [Marquee interrupts] → About → Services → Projects → Interests → Contact
```

This is the problem. The page is structured like a story told in chronological order of how the site was designed. It's not structured around what a recruiter needs to know, in the order they need to know it.

---

## Where visitors drop off

**Drop 1: After the hero.** The primary CTA is "Let's work together" — vague, low-urgency, no specificity about what "together" means. The secondary CTA is "See the work" which scrolls to Services. That's fine. But there's no third option that I'd expect on any competent portfolio site: **Download CV / Resume**. That's the highest-intent action a recruiter takes and it's not offered anywhere on this page.

**Drop 2: The marquee.** Immediately after the hero, there's a scrolling ticker of keywords. This interrupts the emotional momentum of the hero copy. A recruiter who was nodding along to "I've never been satisfied just describing a market" is now looking at "ECONOMETRICS · STAKEHOLDER MAPPING · STATA & POWER BI" in grey text scrolling past. Jarring tonal shift.

**Drop 3: The About section is too long.** Two paragraphs plus a pull quote plus an education strip. By the time they reach the education strip, most recruiters have already decided whether to move on. The sidebar info (Available, Target Roles, Industries) is the most conversion-relevant content in the section and it's hidden on the right where eyes don't naturally land first.

**Drop 4: Services before Projects.** He has four real projects with specific methods and scale. Recruiters want to see work, not a capabilities list. Projects should come before Services, or Services should be collapsed into a short intro before the projects.

---

## What's missing from a conversion standpoint

1. **No resume download.** This is a P0 miss. Every recruiter I've ever worked with defaults to downloading the CV before reading the site in depth.

2. **No job title in the hero.** "Economics · Data · Policy" is a category, not a position. Compare: "Economics & Data Analyst — available May 2026" tells a recruiter what pile to put this in. The current tag doesn't.

3. **No signal of what companies/institutions are involved.** The text says "Sift Capital," "Cardekho," "Games 24x7," "Park Plus." A recruiter skimming doesn't absorb these names from paragraph text. Company name treatment — even just bolded in-line or listed somewhere visual — would help.

4. **Project cards are dead ends.** Four research projects, none of them have a link, a PDF, a GitHub, anything. The projects section ends with "...what the evidence says actually works." Full stop. A motivated recruiter has no next step.

5. **The contact section CTA copy is confused.** "I'm looking for the right next thing. If you're building it — let's talk." This is fine for tone. But the contact section intro paragraph then explains what he's looking for — essentially repeating the hero. The page is explaining itself instead of converting.

---

## What I'd do

- Add a resume download button in the hero CTAs (third button or replace secondary)
- Move the marquee below the About section, not between hero and about
- Flip the Services and Projects order
- Add company name visual treatment to the services/experience cards
- Wire the form to a real endpoint (Formspree is free)

**Conversion grade: C+.** Good brand, weak conversion architecture.
